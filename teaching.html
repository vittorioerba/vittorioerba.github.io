Variance in agent-based dynamics on graphs

Bachelor thesis (Tesi triennale)
Computational (agent-based simulations on graphs) | Theoretical (Markov chains)

Agent-based simulations are widely used to model complex systems where the collective behaviour of the degrees of freedom is determined by the interaction of many simple individual units, called agents; for example, they are used to model epidemics. These simulations are intrinsically stochastic, meaning that the temporal evolution of the system is not fully determined by its initial state. This raises a fundamental question: is the average temporal evolution of the system a good estimator for the behaviour of a single instance of the temporal evolution? In other words: can I predict the future state of my system accurately if I know its average temporal evolution? Are the stochastic fluctuations too large to rule out the possibility of making reliable predictions?

The project will focus on understanding the current state of the art of variance estimates in Markovian agent-based models through literature reviews, computer-based simulations and simple Markov-chains computations.

Suggested readings:
  • "Hamiltonian dynamics of the SIS epidemic model with stochastic fluctuations", Nakamura & Martinez (2019)
  • "On methods for studying stochastic disease dynamics", Keeling & Ross (2008)
  • "Variance in system dynamics and agent based modelling using the sir model of infectious disease", Ahmed, Greensmith & Aickelin (2013)
